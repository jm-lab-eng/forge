Project Overview

Development of a robotic system capable of mobilizing and removing sludge from crude oil storage tanks without requiring tank shutdown. The system was designed for operation in hazardous oil & gas environments while improving operational safety and reducing manual tank entry.

Applications

  • Crude oil storage tank maintenance
  • Sludge mobilisation and pumping
  • Reducing manual entry into hazardous tanks
  • Improving operational safety in oil & gas facilities

My Engineering Contributions

  • Designed the piston pump subsystem for sludge mobilisation and pumping
  • Developed hydraulic circuit architecture and selected components for hazardous environments
  • Performed hydraulic calculations: flow estimation, pressure losses, system performance analysis
  • Conducted structural analysis to support subsystem design decisions
  • Designed inductive proximity-sensor based position sensing for hydraulic cylinder control
  • Participated in DFMA / DFX design reviews and recommended design improvements
  • Performed engineering risk assessments: ignition hazard analysis, DFMEA, Job Safety Analysis
  • Coordinated engineering activities during prototype development, testing, and field validation

Key Learnings

  • Exposure to engineering development for oil & gas industry applications
  • Understanding of hazardous area engineering and explosion protection principles
  • Practical experience in hydraulic system design and subsystem integration
  • Engineering decision making through iterative prototyping and testing
  • Working with multidisciplinary teams across design, fabrication and testing
