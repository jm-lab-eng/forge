DRACO

In-Service Crude Oil Tank Cleaning Robot

Development of a robotic system designed to perform crude oil tank cleaning while tanks remain operational, improving safety and reducing operational downtime in hazardous environments.

This case study summarizes my engineering contributions during the development of the DRACO system. Information presented here is based on publicly available details and personal engineering experience without disclosing confidential project information.

Project Overview

DRACO is a robotic system developed for in-service crude oil tank cleaning. The project focused on enabling sludge mobilisation and removal without tank shutdown while improving operational safety in hazardous environments.

Key Engineering Challenges

  • Operating within confined tank environments with limited access.
  • Designing equipment suitable for hazardous oil & gas environments.
  • Handling complex sludge behaviour and fluid interactions.
  • Ensuring reliability for real industrial deployment.
  • Integrating mechanical, hydraulic, and sensing subsystems in a compact platform.

Engineering Development Journey

Feasibility & Problem Study

Studied crude oil tank cleaning operations, sludge behaviour, and hazardous environment constraints to evaluate feasibility of a robotic cleaning solution.

Concept Development

Explored multiple robotic architectures, propulsion concepts, and sludge pumping approaches before selecting a feasible system configuration.

Subsystem Design

Designed key subsystems including propulsion system, piston pump mechanism, sludge mobilisation systems, and hydraulic circuits.

Simulation & Engineering Analysis

Performed hydraulic studies, propulsion calculations, structural analysis, and virtual testing of pump and propeller configurations.

Prototype Development

Developed scaled piston pump prototypes and subsystem models to validate engineering concepts experimentally.

Experimental Testing

Built engineering test setups including propeller thrust rigs and piston pump testing systems to evaluate subsystem performance.

Integrated System Testing

Developed in-house tank testing setup to evaluate propulsion, pumping, and subsystem integration under simulated conditions.

Field Validation

Supported system deployment and field validation to assess operational performance in real tank environments.

Engineering Contributions & Learnings

  • Studied explosion protection requirements including ATEX Directive 2014/34/EU, PESO recommendations, and IEC / IS 60079 & 80079 series standards.
  • Contributed to mechanical architecture and subsystem layout of the robotic system.
  • Selected materials and manufacturing approaches suitable for crude oil and hazardous environments.
  • Designed hydraulic circuit architecture and selected hydraulic components for pumping and actuation systems.
  • Performed engineering calculations for propulsion mechanisms and hydraulic system performance.
  • Conducted structural analysis to support mechanical design decisions.
  • Led development of a scaled prototype piston pump for sludge mobilisation studies.
  • Designed inductive proximity sensor based position sensing for hydraulic cylinder control.
  • Supported subsystem integration, testing, and field validation activities.
  • Later led engineering efforts across design, prototype development, testing, and field implementation.

Project Outcome

The project resulted in the development of a robotic system designed to enable safer and more efficient crude oil tank cleaning operations while reducing operational disruption and minimizing human exposure to hazardous environments.
