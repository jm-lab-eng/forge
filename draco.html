DRACO

In-Service Crude Oil Tank Cleaning Robot

A multi-year engineering program focused on developing a robotic system capable of cleaning crude oil storage tanks while in service, improving safety and reducing operational downtime.

Project Overview

DRACO is a robotic system developed for in-service crude oil tank cleaning. The project focused on enabling sludge mobilisation and removal without tank shutdown while improving operational safety in hazardous environments.

Key Engineering Challenges

  • Operating within confined tank environments with limited access.
  • Designing equipment suitable for hazardous oil & gas environments.
  • Handling complex sludge behaviour and fluid interactions.
  • Ensuring reliability for real industrial deployment.
  • Integrating mechanical, hydraulic, and sensing subsystems in a compact platform.

Development Lifecycle

Feasibility → Concept Development → System & Detailed Design → Prototype Development → Testing & Validation → Field Deployment

The development of the system progressed through multiple engineering phases beginning with feasibility studies and concept exploration, followed by subsystem design, prototype development, testing, and field deployment. Each stage involved iterative refinement and validation to ensure reliable system performance under real operating conditions.

Key Engineering Areas

  • Mechanical system architecture
  • Hydraulic system design and integration
  • Structural analysis and engineering calculations
  • Prototype development and iterative design refinement
  • System integration and field validation

  • Performed engineering calculations for propulsion mechanisms and hydraulic system performance.

Project Outcome

The project resulted in the development of a robotic system designed to enable safer and more efficient crude oil tank cleaning operations while reducing operational disruption and minimizing human exposure to hazardous environments.
