DRACO

In-Service Crude Oil Tank Cleaning Robot

A multi-year engineering effort focused on developing a robotic system capable of cleaning crude oil storage tanks while in service, enabling safer maintenance and reducing operational downtime.

Project Overview

Crude oil storage tanks accumulate sludge over time, reducing storage capacity and creating operational challenges. Traditional cleaning methods typically require tank shutdown and manual entry into hazardous environments.

The DRACO project focused on developing a robotic system capable of performing sludge mobilization and cleaning operations without requiring tank shutdown. The system was designed to operate within confined tank environments while meeting strict safety and reliability requirements.

Engineering Challenges

  • Operating inside large industrial tanks with limited access points
  • Designing systems suitable for hazardous industrial environments
  • Handling complex sludge characteristics and fluid behaviour
  • Ensuring mechanical reliability in demanding field conditions
  • Integrating multiple subsystems into a compact robotic platform

Development Lifecycle

Feasibility → Concept Development → System & Detailed Design → Prototype Development → Testing & Validation → Field Deployment

The system was developed through multiple engineering phases, beginning with feasibility studies and concept exploration, followed by subsystem design, prototype development, and validation through both controlled testing and field deployment.

Key Engineering Areas

  • Mechanical system architecture and design
  • Hydraulic system integration
  • Structural design and engineering analysis
  • Prototype development and design iteration
  • System integration and validation testing

My Role & Contributions

I was involved from the early stages of the project and later took responsibility for leading key aspects of the mechanical development and execution phases. My work spanned the project lifecycle from early design exploration through prototype development and field validation.

  • Mechanical architecture and subsystem design
  • 3D CAD modelling and engineering documentation
  • Hydraulic system integration
  • Structural design and engineering analysis
  • Prototype development and testing

Testing & Validation

Multiple prototype iterations were developed and evaluated through controlled testing environments before field deployment. These testing activities focused on verifying system reliability, operational performance, and subsystem integration.

Dedicated in-house testing setups were also developed to simulate operational conditions and support iterative improvements to the system design.

Project Outcome

The project resulted in the successful development of a robotic system designed to enable safer and more efficient tank cleaning operations while minimizing operational disruption.
